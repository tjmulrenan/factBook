Grab Bag of Greatness 🎁 (fallback)



1. “Draw Your Favorite Fact!”
Kids are prompted to illustrate the fact they liked most from the book.

Simple prompt with space to draw.

Optional: mini icons or fun prompts like “Add a dinosaur even if it didn’t belong!”

2. “Guess the Year!”
Multiple facts are shown without the year.

Kids have to choose which year they think each fact happened in (from a short list).

Encourages time-based reasoning and memory.

3. “True or False?”
Fun mix of real and made-up facts.

Kids guess if each one is true or false.

Correct answers can be revealed in the next page or upside down.

4. “Match the Fact to the Tag!”
A set of facts and a set of tags (like “sports”, “space”, “music”).

Kids draw lines or connect which fact goes with which theme.

5. “Fact Remix”
Kids pick any fact and rewrite it in their own words or draw a comic strip version.

Encourages creativity and comprehension.

6. “Factbook Mad Libs”
Take a real fact and blank out a few words (person, place, action).

Kids fill in silly or serious replacements for laughs.

7. “Pick Your Team!”
Several famous figures from the book are listed.

Kids choose 3–5 for their "dream team" and say why (e.g., “I want Marie Curie because she’s brainy!”).

8. “Factbook Awards”
Kids vote on:

Funniest Fact

Weirdest Moment

Most Inspiring Person

Best Invention

Gives a wrap-up feel and helps recall.

9. “Design a Cover!”
A template where they draw a new cover for the book.

Encourages personalization and ownership.
