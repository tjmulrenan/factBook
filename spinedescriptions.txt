January – Snowflakes & Mittens
Captures the cold start of the year. Abstract mitten silhouettes + flakes.

February – Books & Keys
“Treasures of knowledge” — like unlocking facts. Gives February a special “unlocking” feel rather than Valentine’s clichés.

March – Clovers & Rain Boots
Spring arriving → clovers and playful boot outlines (puddle season).

April – Umbrellas & Raindrops
Heavy April showers → clean umbrella + drop icons.

May – Blossoms & Birds
New life everywhere. 5-petal flower silhouettes + simple bird shapes.

June – Sunglasses & Suns
Early summer feel. Not just generic “sun” → add fun sunglass shapes.

July – Kites & Clouds
Windy midsummer skies. Kids flying kites, very global.

August – Sandcastles & Seashells
Late summer / holiday feel → castle towers + spiral shell outlines.

September – Apples & Pencils
Back-to-school → classic and recognizable for kids everywhere.

October – Pumpkins & Owls
Seasonal but not just “Halloween” → works for harvest + autumn.

November – Falling Leaves & Mushrooms
Bare trees + mushrooms popping up → deep autumn, cozy.

December – Stars & Pine Trees
Wintry but not too “Christmas-only” → triangles as trees + little star bursts.
